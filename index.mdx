## What is Lucky Roll Dice?

Lucky Roll Dice is a two-player browser-based game built with vanilla JavaScript, HTML, and CSS. Two players take turns rolling a six-sided die, racing to reach **100 points** first. Every roll is a gamble — accumulate points greedily, but if you roll a **1**, you lose everything accumulated in that turn.

## How the game works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start a new game">
    Click **NEW GAME** to reset all scores. Player 1 always goes first, indicated by the red dot next to their name.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Roll the dice">
    Click **ROLL DICE** to roll. If the result is 2–6, the points are added to your current score. If you roll a **1**, your current score resets to 0 and the turn passes to your opponent.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Hold your score">
    Click **HOLD** to bank your current score into your global total. The turn then passes to your opponent.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Win the game">
    The first player to reach **100 global points** wins. A browser alert announces the winner and the game reloads.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The game is served by a lightweight Express.js server and requires Node.js to run locally. See the [setup guide](/local/setup) for instructions.
</Note>
