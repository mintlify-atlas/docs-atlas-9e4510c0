Lucky Roll Dice is a two-player game played with a single six-sided die. Players alternate turns, accumulating points and deciding when to bank them. The first player to reach **100 global points** wins.

## Players and the turn indicator

Each game involves exactly two players — **Player 1** and **Player 2**. A red dot (a FontAwesome circle icon, color `#eb4d4c`) appears next to the active player's name to show whose turn it is.

When you click **NEW GAME**, all scores reset to 0 and the red dot is placed next to Player 1's name, giving them the first turn.

<Warning>
  You must click **NEW GAME** before rolling or holding. Without it, the red dot has no position and the game has no active player — the roll and hold buttons will not function correctly.
</Warning>

## Actions available on your turn

On your turn, you have three buttons available:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="NEW GAME" icon="rotate-right">
    Resets all global and current scores to 0 and places the turn indicator on Player 1. Use this at the start of every match.
  </Card>
  <Card title="ROLL DICE" icon="dice">
    Generates a random result between 1 and 6. The outcome either adds to your current score or ends your turn immediately.
  </Card>
  <Card title="HOLD" icon="hand">
    Banks your current score into your global total, resets your current score to 0, and passes the turn to your opponent.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How a turn works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start your turn">
    The red dot appears next to your name. Your current score displays 0 (or carries over from the turn start — it is always reset after a hold or a roll of 1).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Roll the die">
    Click **ROLL DICE**. A random integer from 1 to 6 is generated using:

    ```javascript
    function generateRandomInt(min = 0, max = 6) {
      min = Math.ceil(min);
      max = Math.floor(max);
      return Math.floor(Math.random() * (max - min)) + min;
    }
    ```

    The die face rendered on screen shows the result as a dot pattern.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Check the result">
    Two outcomes are possible:

    - **Rolled 2–6** — the number is added to your current score. You remain in your turn and can roll again or hold.
    - **Rolled 1** — your current score resets to 0 and your turn ends immediately. Play passes to your opponent.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Decide: roll again or hold">
    If you rolled 2–6, you choose:

    - **Roll again** — take the risk of losing your accumulated current score if you roll a 1.
    - **Hold** — safely add your current score to your global total, then pass the turn.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Turn ends">
    Your turn ends either by rolling a 1 (forced) or clicking **HOLD** (voluntary). The red dot moves to your opponent and their turn begins.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## The roll-1 penalty

<Warning>
  Rolling a 1 immediately resets your current score to 0 and passes the turn to your opponent. You lose every point accumulated in that turn — your global score is not affected, but all unbanked progress is gone.
</Warning>

This is enforced directly in `keepRoundScore`:

```javascript
function keepRoundScore(cs) {
  let roundScore = parseInt(cs.textContent) + randomNumber;
  const currentPlayer = checkDotPlayerPosition();
  if (currentPlayer === "player1" && randomNumber === 1) {
    cs.textContent = "0";
    player2.append(dotPlayer); // pass turn to player2
    return 0;
  }
  if (currentPlayer === "player2" && randomNumber === 1) {
    cs.textContent = "0";
    player1.append(dotPlayer); // pass turn to player1
    return 0;
  }
  cs.textContent = roundScore;
  return roundScore;
}
```

## Holding with 0 current score

The **HOLD** button is always active — you can click it even when your current score is 0. Doing so simply adds 0 to your global score and passes the turn. There is no benefit to holding a 0, so avoid it unless you want to give your opponent the turn.

<Tip>
  If you roll a 1 and the turn passes automatically, there is no need to click **HOLD**. The turn has already ended and your opponent is now active.
</Tip>

## Strategy hints

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="The risk vs. reward trade-off">
    Every roll beyond your first is a gamble. The probability of rolling a 1 on any single roll is 1 in 6 (approximately 17%). The longer you stay in your turn, the more you stand to lose. A common stopping point is a current score of 15–20 per turn, which keeps steady progress without excessive exposure.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Watch your opponent's global score">
    If your opponent is close to 100, you may need to take more risk per turn to catch up. Conversely, if you are ahead, conservative play — holding early — limits opportunities for your opponent to overtake you.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="The opening turn advantage">
    Player 1 always goes first. Player 2 has the advantage of knowing Player 1's global score before deciding when to hold on their own turn. Use this information to calibrate risk.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
