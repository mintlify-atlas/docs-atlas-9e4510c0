Lucky Roll Dice tracks two separate score values for each player at all times: a **current score** and a **global score**. Understanding the difference between them is essential to playing well.

## Two score types

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Current score" icon="clock">
    Displayed in the **CURRENT** panel on each player's side. Accumulates within a single turn. Resets to 0 if you roll a 1 or after you hold.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Global score" icon="trophy">
    Displayed as the large red number above the player's name. Persists across turns. Only increases when you click **HOLD**.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Score initialization

When you click **NEW GAME**, all four score values reset to 0:

```javascript
function beginGame() {
  globalScore1.textContent = "0";
  globalScore2.textContent = "0";
  currentScore1.textContent = "0";
  currentScore2.textContent = "0";
  player1.append(dotPlayer); // Player 1 goes first
}
```

No score from a previous game carries over.

## How current score accumulates

Each time you roll a 2–6, the result is added to your current score:

```javascript
function keepRoundScore(cs) {
  let roundScore = parseInt(cs.textContent) + randomNumber;
  // ...
  cs.textContent = roundScore;
  return roundScore;
}
```

The formula is straightforward:

```text
currentScore = currentScore + dieResult
```

For example, if your current score is 7 and you roll a 5, your current score becomes 12.

<Warning>
  Rolling a 1 sets your current score back to 0 and ends your turn. You do not get to keep any points accumulated that turn.
</Warning>

## How global score updates

When you click **HOLD**, your current score is added to your global score:

```javascript
function keepGlobalScore(cs, gs, player) {
  let mainScore = parseInt(cs.textContent) + parseInt(gs.textContent);
  gs.textContent = mainScore;
  cs.textContent = 0;
  player.append(dotPlayer);
  // win check follows...
}
```

The formula:

```text
globalScore = globalScore + currentScore
currentScore = 0
```

After the hold, your current score resets to 0 and the turn passes to your opponent.

<Note>
  Your global score only ever increases — it can never decrease during a game. Only your current score is at risk on each roll.
</Note>

## Worked example

The table below traces a sample game for Player 1 across several turns.

| Event | Die result | Current score | Global score |
|---|---|---|---|
| Game starts | — | 0 | 0 |
| Roll | 4 | 4 | 0 |
| Roll | 3 | 7 | 0 |
| Roll | 5 | 12 | 0 |
| **Hold** | — | 0 | **12** |
| Roll | 6 | 6 | 12 |
| **Hold** | — | 0 | **18** |
| Roll | 2 | 2 | 18 |
| Roll | 5 | 7 | 18 |
| Roll | 1 | **0** (reset) | 18 |
| Roll (next turn) | 6 | 6 | 18 |
| Roll | 4 | 10 | 18 |
| **Hold** | — | 0 | **28** |

Key observations from the example:

- Rolling a 1 wipes the current score (turn 8–9) but leaves the global score untouched at 18.
- Each **HOLD** commits exactly what is in the current score at that moment — no more, no less.
- The global score grows only at hold events, not during rolling.

## Score display locations

| Element | HTML ID | What it shows |
|---|---|---|
| Player 1 global score | `#GS1` | Player 1's banked total |
| Player 2 global score | `#GS2` | Player 2's banked total |
| Player 1 current score | `#CS1` | Player 1's unbanked turn score |
| Player 2 current score | `#CS2` | Player 2's unbanked turn score |

<Tip>
  Keep an eye on both your current score and your opponent's global score simultaneously. A large current score is only valuable once you hold it — until then, it is at risk.
</Tip>
